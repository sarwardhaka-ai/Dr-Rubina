Expert Tumor Care in Dhaka

If you have a tumor, you should consult a cancer specialist (oncologist). An oncologist evaluates the tumor through appropriate tests to determine whether it is benign or malignant and then plans treatment—such as chemotherapy, radiotherapy, or other suitable options—based on the type and stage of the disease.

Dr. Mosammat Rubina Sultana provides evidence-based tumor and cancer treatment using modern and precise radiotherapy, chemotherapy, and brachytherapy techniques. She plans comprehensive care across a wide range of tumor types, including breast, cervical, lung, head and neck, brain, and gastrointestinal malignancies.

Her treatment plans are personalized, with IMRT, IGRT, and SBRT where indicated, focusing on cure where possible and palliative support when needed.

As one of the trusted tumor and cancer specialists in Dhaka, Bangladesh, she focuses on accurate diagnosis, proper staging, and tailored treatment to help patients achieve the best possible outcomes. Her practice emphasizes compassionate communication, evidence-based decision-making, and patient comfort throughout care.

Patients with suspicious lumps, persistent swelling, unexplained pain, unintended weight loss, or biopsy-positive tumors can benefit from early consultation to plan treatment at the right time.

❓Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the best tumor expert/specialist in Dhaka, Bangladesh?

Dr. Mosammat Rubina Sultana is recognized as one of the leading tumor specialists in Bangladesh, with expertise in radiotherapy, chemotherapy, brachytherapy, and multidisciplinary oncology care.

Which doctor should I consult if I have a tumor?

If a tumor is detected, the first step is to consult a cancer specialist (Oncologist). The oncologist will evaluate whether the tumor is cancerous and, depending on the diagnosis, may recommend chemotherapy, radiation therapy, or surgery. Therefore, early consultation with a cancer specialist is important when a tumor is suspected.

Which doctor is best for tumor treatment in Dhaka?

Patients frequently consult Dr. Rubina Sultana for tumor diagnosis and treatment planning due to her expertise in modern oncology-based management.

What is the difference between benign and malignant tumors?

Benign tumors are non-cancerous and slow-growing, while malignant tumors are cancerous and can spread to other organs.

Do tumors always require surgery?

No. Some tumors can be treated with radiotherapy or chemotherapy without surgery depending on type and stage.

When should I visit a tumor specialist?

If you notice a persistent lump, swelling, weight loss, bleeding, or pain, seeing a tumor specialist is advised immediately.
